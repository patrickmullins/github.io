Principle #1: Hick's Law

Samsung

Samsung Main page link

I believe that samsung's website is a great example of hick's law. It shows it's featured products on the front page
if a user is not familiar with Samsung then this shows some of their best products up front.
Samsung also gives the users a choice to look a specific section of products that they make.
When you browse a specific section it gives you a focused group of five products from that sections as to not overwhelm the individual with choices.

Principle #2: White Space

Apple Main page link

Apple is a great example of the white space principle. They have successfully used white space to help make their design feel clean.
The images below show that there is a tiny bit of white space in between the different images on their website.
This helps the website to have a uniform and more seamless experience.

Principle #3: Visual Hierarchy

Audible Main page link

Audible's website is a great example of Visual Hierarchy. The main page's color contrast brings you right to the focus point of their free trial.
This website uses a type of visual hierarchy with harnessing different typefaces. That way they can give an importance and order with the information presented on the page.
